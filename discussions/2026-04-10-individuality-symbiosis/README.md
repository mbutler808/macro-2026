## Individuality, Symbiosis and Selection

Marguilis and Fester (1991) is a highly cited book, and this opening chapter makes some provacative claims that got some very strong reaction at the time. The middle is a bit of a rant, but pay attention to the beginning and the end of this essay. Maynard-Smith (1991) is informative for providing a Darwinan view.

Gibert et al 2012 reexamines the concept of individuality from a symbiotic perspective. The first half of the paper is very interesting for the review of symbiosis with respect to major functional systems of animals, but for this course please focus on the evolutionary implications in the second half of the paper.  

39. __Margulis, L. (1991)__. Symbiogenesis and Symbionticism. In Symbiosis as a Source
of Evolutionary Innovation: Speciation and Morphogenesis (ed. L. Margulis and R.
Fester), pp. 1-14. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press.  __map:Taren__
