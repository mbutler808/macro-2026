## Phylogenetics Models

Two developments were key to the explosion of phylogenetic methods that we have today.  The first was molecular sequencing. The first technology was protein sequencing, which involved purifying protein, digesting into smaller fragments, labelling, and separating out the fragments in overlapping arrays. So the first molecular sequences the world had were protein sequences developed by Frederick Sanger in the 1950s, before he went on to develop DNA sequencing (a.k.a. Sanger sequencing)!  The second was the availability of computers, at first mainframes and then PCs. I guess a third was the development of PCR technology so that basically any biologist can generate sequence data.  The first reading is a survey of the approaches and their development, the second reading is a survey of models of DNA evolution.

The second reading for today introduces the models of DNA evolution. Now that we have a very specific phenotype, it is possible to generate a model for how DNA might evolve. What is the frequency of A -> T mutations, etc.? How can this be used to puzzle out the phylogeny from a dataset of sequences?
