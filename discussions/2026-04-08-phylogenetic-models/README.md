## Models of DNA Evolution

The first reading covers the early development of molecular phylogenetic methods, and is a fun read. It is a great summary of parsimony and distance methods and philosophical differences. Think about this as you ponder the implicit assumptions about characters, homology, individuality, and what we are actually estimating with a phylogeny. 

Here are some \[[notes](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1_1Pk_apn52n0IibvKzE78gsirtYb3yx58QSz9Zt9xYk/edit?tab=t.0)\] developed by a previous class

The second reading is a detailed run through of models of DNA evolution, in a mathematically explicit (mechanistic) way. It requires some effort, but once it becomes clear it is beautifully simple, and is well worth the effort for the deeper understanding you will achieve.
