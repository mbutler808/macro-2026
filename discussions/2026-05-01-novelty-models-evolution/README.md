## Readerʻs Choice

Each member of the class is invited to select a paper to read and present to the class. 

Please choose a paper describing the __evolution of a character hypothesized to evolve via some of the special mechanisms__ we have read this semester ( changes to the development via modules or switches, heterocrony/heterotopy, symbiosis and microbiome, phenotpyic plasticity, etc.). 

Please load your to the \[[google drive](https://drive.google.com/drive/folders/18p2-gc7O_cJSvID6beP_6fBqZRhM7zf-?usp=share_link)\] so that everyone can have a look if interested (add your name to the filename. No obligation to read the other papers. 

At class, be prepared to share: 

1. Brief summary of the paper: a sentence each on: what was known before -> the hypothesis, the data/how they approached the question, and what they found.  

2. How rapidly did the character evolve? Is the phenotype better described by gradual evolution or punctuational evolution? (why)

3. Would you expect a phylogenetic comparative analysis of this character to be better fit by a Brownian Motion model? Or an Ornstein-Uhlenbeck model of evolution (sensu Butler and King 2004)? What would the interpretation of the model and its parameters describe in terms of the evolution and evolvability of the character? 

4. Answer questions about your paper.
