# Zool719:  Macroevolution (3cr)
#### Spring 2026 WF 10:30-11:45am  CRN: 89387 ART 137
#### email mbutler808@gmail.com for info. Approved for EECB


This is a graduate course exploring how macroevolutionary ideas around the
generation of biodiversity are changing with new ideas on the
roles of development, epigenetic mechanisms, and microbiome
that expand evolutionary possibilities beyond the Modern
Synthesis.

We will conduct close reading of major papers in evolutionary biology to sharpen critical thinking skills and deepen understanding of the major ideas in the field of macroevolution, beginning with Darwin, the Modern Synthesis, and papers guided by the new book *Evolution Evolving: The Developmental Origins of Adaptation and Biodiversity*. 

### __Mechanics:__  
The course will meet twice weekly WF 10:30-11:45am. Each session will involve a reading and small group discussion. Students will demonstrate understanding in take-home midterms on the readings. Grading: Students will be graded 25% on participation, and 75% on 3 midterms.

### __Topics:__
1. Darwinʻs revolution
2. Consolidation of the Modern Synthesis and ideas on macroevolutionary diversification
3. Challenges to the traditional understanding of heredity and evolution
4. Evolution and the general principles of development
5. The organism and its role in finding adaptive solutions
6. Evolution by phenotypic plasticity
7. The role of developmental bias in shaping evolutionary potential
8. Expanding the causes of selection and dynamics of adaptive evolution
9. Extra-genetic inheritance: parental eﬀects and cultural evolution
10. Extra-genetic inheritance: epigenetics and the microbiome
11. Development and the evolution of novelty
12. The origins of evolvability
13. A new structure of evolutionary theory
